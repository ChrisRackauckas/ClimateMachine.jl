CLIMA

CLIMA is a software package that models the evolution of the Earth system over weeks to centuries. CLIMA solves partial differential equations that conserve the three-dimensional distributions of water, momentum, energy, and tracers such as carbon between the Earth's atmosphere, ocean, and land surface.

CLIMA's purpose is to harness as much data as possible to predict droughts, heat waves, rainfall extremes, and the evolution of Earth's climate.

Subcomponents

CLIMA couples three models for the subcomponents of the Earth system:

  • CLIMA.Atmos: A model for the motion, transfer of solar radiation, and distribution of water, heat, and other tracers in the Earth's atmosphere.
  • CLIMA.Ocean: A model for the motion and distribution of temperature, salinity, and other tracers in the Earth's ocean.
  • CLIMA.Land: A model for the transfer of carbon, water, heat, and solar radiation in the Earth's terrestrial biosphere.

The subcomponents are coupled by exchanging water, momentum, energy, and tracers across their boundaries.

Dynamical core

A dynamical core based on discontinuous Galerkin numerical methods is used to discretize the physical balances that underlie each of CLIMA's subcomponents.

Installation

Installing CLIMA requires julia 1.3.1 and MPI. See Installing CLIMA for more information.

Authors

CLIMA is being developed by the Climate Modeling Alliance.
